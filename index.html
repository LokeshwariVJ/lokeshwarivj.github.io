About Me

Quality isn't a phase —
it's a discipline.

I started my career focused on catching defects. Over time, I've come to see quality engineering as something far more consequential: understanding product risk deeply, partnering across teams early, and building the release confidence that lets organizations move fast without breaking trust.

With 9+ years across payments ecosystems, cloud applications, geospatial mapping, and API-driven services, I've built a broad technical foundation — from Cypress and Selenium automation to GraphQL validation, ETL data quality, k6 performance benchmarking, and CI/CD integration.

My experience spans high-stakes environments: financial transaction flows at TCS working on PayPal-aligned systems, e-commerce automation at Plexus Worldwide, and geospatial mapping platform QA at KUBRA. Each context sharpened my instincts about what quality really means when it matters most.

Currently based in Chandler, AZ, I'm actively exploring senior QA and SDET opportunities — with a strong interest in relocating to San Jose or the broader California tech ecosystem.

Projects That Delivered

Selected case studies where testing strategy, automation engineering, and cross-team collaboration produced real, measurable results.

01
Cypress Automation Framework — Geospatial Mapping Platform (KUBRA)
Challenge
Storm Center and mapping applications required comprehensive UI and API automation coverage — but existing testing was largely manual, regression cycles were long, and geospatial UI behaviors were difficult to validate systematically.
What I Did
Built and maintained Cypress frameworks covering both UI and API layers. Designed reusable components, custom command libraries, and test data strategies suited to geospatial product complexity. Mentored team members on framework patterns and Git workflows, multiplying the team's automation capability.
Impact
Manual regression effort reduced by 40%. Coverage expanded across mapping layers and service integrations, giving the team genuine release confidence on each cycle.

02
GraphQL API Test Suite — Complex Service Validation
Challenge
GraphQL service interactions at KUBRA lacked structured coverage, creating uncertainty around data accuracy, response correctness, and end-to-end workflow integrity.
What I Did
Designed and executed comprehensive Postman test suites covering queries, mutations, error conditions, and user-facing workflows. Integrated with CI for ongoing validation and shared collections with development teams.
Impact
API defects surfaced earlier in the release cycle. Postman collections adopted as shared QA and developer artifacts, improving cross-team alignment and test consistency.
PostmanGraphQLAPI Validation
03
Automation Suite Recovery — Pass Rate Turnaround
Challenge
At Plexus Worldwide, the Selenium automation suite had degraded to a 64.77% pass rate — making CI results untrustworthy and slowing delivery teams who couldn't rely on the pipeline.
What I Did
Conducted systematic failure analysis across all failing tests, identified root causes — flaky selectors, stale test data, timing dependencies — and applied targeted fixes while restoring CI integration stability.
Impact
Pass rate rose from 64.77% to 75.19% in two months. The pipeline became a reliable quality signal again — and the team regained confidence in automated test results.

Leadership & Approach

More Than a Tester.

Quality engineering at its best is a strategic discipline. Here's how I show up — for teams, products, and the people I work alongside.

🔍
Risk Before Test Cases
I start every engagement by understanding what failure would mean for the customer and the business. That context — especially in payments and high-trust products — shapes everything about where I invest testing effort.
🧩
Embedded in Agile Teams
I contribute in sprint planning, design discussions, and defect triage — not just test execution. Quality thinking applied upstream consistently leads to cleaner code, clearer requirements, and fewer late surprises.
📈
Metrics That Drive Decisions
Pass rates, regression coverage, defect escape rates — I use these as navigation, not decoration. They tell me where to invest next, and how to make the case for quality investment to leadership.
🎓
Mentoring with Intention
At KUBRA I mentored QA team members on Cypress design patterns, Git workflows, and test data strategy — helping them grow technically and gain confidence in building automation that lasts.
💼
Speaking the Language of Risk
I communicate with product owners, engineering leads, and stakeholders in terms they care about — release confidence, business risk, and customer impact — not just test counts and bug lists.
🌱
Continuous Improvement Mindset
I treat QA processes the way a good engineer treats code: always open to refactoring. Whether it's recovering a broken pipeline or redesigning a test strategy, I look for what's slowing the team down and fix it.
